refract Math3D

computes the direction of a refracted ray if i specifies the normalized(!) direction of the incoming ray and n specifies the normalized(!) normal vector of the interface of two optical media (e.g. air and water). The vector n should point to the side from where i is coming, i.e. the dot product of n and i should be negative. The floating-point number r is the ratio of the refractive index of the medium from where the ray comes to the refractive index of the medium on the other side of the surface. Thus, if a ray comes from air (refractive index about 1.0) and hits the surface of water (refractive index 1.33), then the ratio r is 1.0 / 1.33 = 0.75.
